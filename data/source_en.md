# Three-Body and Hou Yi

The Trisolaran crisis had entered its final phase. The simulation on the giant screen was a vortex of despair: three massive stars, in a chaotic gravitational dance, tore at the planet. The predicted "Triple Swift" was imminent; the planet would be torn apart by opposing gravitational tides, and its core would dissolve into plasma within hours. Dehydration was useless; the storage centers were already igniting.

The Princeps stared at the countdown. "Total annihilation. The mathematical tools of our entire civilization are garbage."

"We need a dynamic solution, not a computational one," the Science Consul said. "Our sophons, while monitoring the primitive world in the next spiral arm, detected a significant quantum anomaly in their remote history. Their collective consciousness holds a vector potential, a mathematical seed known as Hou Yi."

The Consul gestured, and a visualization appeared: "This entity is not merely a myth. According to the data, Hou Yi represents a civilization that, at its technological infancy, encountered a multi-star event. In a desperate act of mass ritualistic intent, they projected a collective conscious field that catalyzed a localized vacuum collapse, neutralizing the 'extra' celestial bodies. It's a localized, low-probability quantum ballistic solution."

"A myth?" the Princeps scoffed. "And you want us to perform a ritual?"

"No, Princeps. We synthesize the vector. We don't summon a god; we implement the algorithm of their legend."

The project, designated "The Hou Yi Solar Decommissioning", initiated. Using high-dimensional unfolded proton memory and the planet’s remaining neural networks, the Trisolarans extrapolated the will of Hou Yi. They didn't build a body; they projected a mathematical construct of pure force and intentionality, calibrated to Earth's legendary coordinates.

The construct materialized. It was a terrifying fusion of ancient terrestrial biology and crystalline sophon matter. It held no bow, but a lattice of strange matter. Its eyes did not see light, but the curvature of spacetime. It looked at the three roaring suns and calculated not their mass, but their mathematical doom.

The First Kinetic Stroke:
The entity drew its strange-matter lattice. There was no sound, only the abrupt silent release of a localized singularity. The smallest, most volatile star, which had been whipping near the planet, instantly inverted. Its core collapsed not into a supernova, but into a micro-black hole, which evaporated within a Planck second, taking the chaotic orbital energy with it. The planetary orbit stabilized.

The Second Kinetic Stroke:
The second, larger star was in a destructive resonance with the remaining sun, preparing to accelerate and deliver a crushing blow. The construct focused. Its entire high-dimensional matrix aligned. It didn't release a projectile; it emitted a directed burst of localized negative entropy. The arrow of time within the star was, momentarily, reversed. The nuclear furnace stalled. Gravity took over, crushing the star into a small, dense white dwarf. Its gravitational pull ceased. It drifted, harmless, to the periphery.

The construct did not look back. Having fulfilled the protocol, it dissolved back into the sophon-memory banks.

One sun remained. A perfect, yellow star, now locked into a stable, non-chaotic orbit. The light that fell upon the charred planet was steady and predictable. For the first time in an eon, the three-body problem was solved. The Era of Stability was no longer a fleeting chance; it was the new reality.

The Princeps looked up, the single shadow sharp upon the scorched ground. "The primitive's dream solved our reality. Rehydrate the populace. The final 'Era of Stability' has begun."
